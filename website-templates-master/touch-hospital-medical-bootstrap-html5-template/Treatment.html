Acne Vulgaris

  • Very common globally
  • Types: blackheads, whiteheads, nodules, cystic acne
  • Linked to cosmetic & medical concerns

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)

  • Common inflammatory condition
  • Dry, red, itchy patches
  • Chronic, seasonal flares

Psoriasis

  • Chronic autoimmune disease
  • Red plaques with silvery scales
  • Common on elbows, knees, scalp

Fungal Infections (Dermatophytosis, Ringworm, Tinea)

  • Prevalent in tropical climates
  • Circular, red, itchy lesions
  • Easy to identify visually

Vitiligo

  • Autoimmune depigmentation
  • Clear white patches, distinct borders
  • Major cosmetic and psychological impact

Melasma & Hyperpigmentation Disorders

  • Facial dark patches
  • Triggered by sun/hormones
  • Especially common in darker skin tones

Rosacea

  • Chronic facial redness
  • Visible blood vessels, bumps, flushing
  • Often confused with acne

Impetigo

  • Bacterial infection (mainly children)
  • Red sores, crusty blisters around mouth/nose
  • High infection risk, early detection important

Contact Dermatitis

  • Allergic reaction causes red, itchy rash
  • Caused by irritants or allergens
  • Common in all age groups

Actinic Keratosis

  • Rough, scaly patches from sun damage
  • Precancerous skin lesions
  • Early detection can prevent progression to cancer

Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)

  • Pearly bump or non-healing sore
  • Most common skin cancer
  • Rarely spreads but can disfigure

Melanoma

  • Irregular moles, asymmetry, color variation
  • Most dangerous skin cancer
  • Can spread rapidly
