Treatment & Diseases

Acne Vulgaris

A common skin condition with blackheads, whiteheads, pimples, and sometimes cysts.

Treatments

  • Topical: Benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid, retinoids
  • Oral: Antibiotics, isotretinoin (for severe cases)
  • Others: Chemical peels, laser therapy, hormonal treatment

Important: Treatment depends on severity. Consult a dermatologist to avoid scarring.

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)

Chronic inflammatory skin condition causing dry, itchy, red patches.

Treatments

  • Moisturizers and emollients
  • Topical corticosteroids
  • Antihistamines for itching
  • Immunosuppressants for severe cases

Important: Avoid triggers (soaps, allergens). Long-term management is needed.

Psoriasis

Autoimmune disorder with red plaques and silvery scales, often on elbows, knees, and scalp.

Treatments

  • Topical: Vitamin D analogs, corticosteroids
  • Phototherapy
  • Systemic: Methotrexate, cyclosporine, biologics

Important: Requires lifelong care. Treatment depends on severity and affected area.

Fungal Infections (Ringworm, Tinea)

Infections caused by dermatophytes, producing circular, itchy, red patches.

Treatments

  • Topical antifungal creams (clotrimazole, terbinafine)
  • Oral antifungals for resistant cases
  • Hygiene and avoiding sharing clothes/towels

Important: Persistent infections need medical evaluation.

Vitiligo

Autoimmune condition causing white depigmented patches on skin.

Treatments

  • Topical corticosteroids and calcineurin inhibitors
  • Phototherapy (narrowband UVB)
  • Cosmetic camouflage and depigmentation therapy

Important: Treatment focuses on repigmentation, but full recovery may not be possible.

Melasma & Hyperpigmentation

Dark patches on face often due to sun exposure or hormones.

Treatments

  • Sunscreen (broad spectrum)
  • Topical hydroquinone, azelaic acid, kojic acid
  • Chemical peels and laser therapy

Important: Sun protection is essential to prevent recurrence.

Rosacea

Chronic facial redness, visible blood vessels, and acne-like bumps.

Treatments

  • Topical metronidazole, azelaic acid
  • Oral antibiotics (doxycycline)
  • Laser and light-based therapies

Important: Avoid triggers (alcohol, spicy food, heat).

Impetigo

Highly contagious bacterial infection causing crusty sores, common in children.

Treatments

  • Topical antibiotics (mupirocin)
  • Oral antibiotics for widespread cases
  • Good hygiene to prevent spread

Important: Avoid close contact until treated.

Contact Dermatitis

Skin reaction due to allergens or irritants, causing itchy rash.

Treatments

  • Avoid identified triggers
  • Topical corticosteroids
  • Moisturizers to repair skin barrier

Important: Patch testing may help identify allergens.

Actinic Keratosis

Precancerous rough, scaly patches from sun damage.

Treatments

  • Cryotherapy (freezing)
  • Topical 5-fluorouracil or imiquimod
  • Photodynamic therapy

Important: Must be monitored as it can progress to skin cancer.

Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)

Most common skin cancer, slow growing, rarely spreads.

Treatments

  • Surgical excision
  • Cryotherapy
  • Topical or oral targeted therapy

Important: Early removal prevents complications.

Melanoma

The most dangerous skin cancer, can spread rapidly if untreated.

Treatments

  • Surgical excision (early stage)
  • Immunotherapy, targeted therapy, chemotherapy (advanced cases)
  • Regular skin checks and mole monitoring

Important: Early detection saves lives. Seek urgent care if suspicious moles appear.
